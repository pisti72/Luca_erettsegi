Five in a Row

Five in a Row is a simple game where two players take turns placing their pieces on a grid. The first player to get five of their pieces in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins the game.

Game Rules

  • The game is played on a 15x15 grid.
  • Two players take turns placing their pieces on the grid.
  • The first player to get five of their pieces in a row wins the game.
  • If the grid is filled without any player getting five in a row, the game is a draw.

How to Play

To play the game, simply click on an empty cell in the grid to place your piece. The game will automatically check for a winner after each move. If you get five in a row, you win! If the grid is filled without a winner, the game will end in a draw.

Strategy Tips

  • Try to create multiple threats at once to increase your chances of winning.
  • Pay attention to your opponent's moves and try to block their attempts to get five in a row.
  • Control the center of the grid to have more options for placing your pieces.
